# Worm
- A type of malware that is standalone and spreads to other machines by replicating itself. The replication rapidly consumes storage and creates performance issues. In contrast to a [virus](virus.md), worms are triggered without user interaction and are capable of targeted attacks. Worms can be used to distribute and drop other malware such as ransomware. [^1] #glossary
- A computer program which replicates itself and is self- propagating. Worms, as opposed to viruses, are meant to spawn in network environments. Network worms were first defined by Shoch & Hupp of Xerox in ACM Communications (March 1982). The Internet worm of November 1988 is perhaps the most famous; it successfully propagated itself on over 6,000 systems across the Internet. See also: Trojan Horse, virus. [^2] #glossary
