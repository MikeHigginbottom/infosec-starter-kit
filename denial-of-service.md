# Denial Of Service
- [DoS](dos.md) is a malicious attempt to disrupt or block access to and use of the normal traffic of a targeted [server](server.md), service or network by overwhelming the target or its surrounding infrastructure with a flood of Internet traffic. [^3]  It is a violation of [availability](availability.md). [^2] #glossary

- General approach is to bombard a system with connections to keep it so busy that it is unable to accept legitimate connections. [^1]
- A flooding attack sends massive amounts of network traffic to the target overloading the ability of network devices and [servers](server.md) to handle the raw load. [^2]
- A connection exhaustion attack repeatedly makes connection requests to a target to consume all system resources related to connections, which prevents any other connections from being established or maintained. [^2]
- A resource demand attack repeatedly requests a resource from a [server](server.md) in order to keep it too busy to respond to other requests. [^2]
